Doctor: Hello, what brings you in today?
Patient: Doctor, I've been having a burning sensation when I pass urine since the last two days. And I need to go very frequently.
Doctor: Any fever?
Patient: Yes, slight fever. Around 99.5.
Doctor: Any back pain or pain in the sides?
Patient: No, just the lower abdomen feels heavy and uncomfortable.
Doctor: Any blood in the urine?
Patient: No, thankfully.
Doctor: When was your last menstrual period?
Patient: About two weeks ago.
Doctor: Are you sexually active?
Patient: Yes.
Doctor: Any vaginal discharge or itching?
Patient: No, nothing like that.
Doctor: Alright. It sounds like a urinary tract infection, possibly a lower UTI or cystitis. Have you had this before?
Patient: Yes, once about a year ago.
Doctor: Last time, what antibiotic were you given?
Patient: I think it was some tablet for 3 days. I don't remember the name.
Doctor: Okay. I'll prescribe Nitrofurantoin 100mg twice a day for 5 days. Take it with food to avoid stomach upset.
Patient: Okay, doctor.
Doctor: Drink plenty of water — at least 2 to 3 litres a day. This helps flush out the bacteria.
Patient: Should I avoid anything?
Doctor: Avoid caffeine and alcohol for now. And urinate after intercourse — that helps prevent recurrence.
Patient: Alright.
Doctor: If you develop fever above 101, back pain, or see blood in urine, come in immediately. That could mean the infection has moved up to the kidneys.
Patient: Got it, doctor. Thank you.
Doctor: If symptoms don't improve in 2 days, come back and we'll do a urine culture and switch the antibiotic.
Patient: Thank you, doctor.
